## Company Criteria

### Industry Focus â€” Wealth Technology (WealthTech)
The company's core product or platform must serve wealth management, financial advisory, investment management, or adjacent wealth-related functions. This includes:

- Wealthtech SaaS platforms (portfolio management, financial planning, rebalancing, reporting)
- Digital wealth / robo-advisory platforms
- Advisor technology platforms (CRM, client portal, practice management for advisors)
- RIAs and hybrid advisor platforms (registered investment advisors with a tech-forward model)
- Investment management platforms with technology at the core
- Alternative investment / private markets platforms focused on wealth channels
- Embedded wealth / banking-as-a-service companies if wealth management is a primary vertical
- Retirement tech platforms (401k, retirement planning) with a wealth management angle
- Software or Technology companies that have FinTech or Financial Services Vertical

### Company Stage & Size
- **Prioritize startups and growth-stage private companies** (Series B, C, D, and growth-stage)
- **Include public companies** but list them after private companies in the output
- Within each category, sort by perceived relevance and company momentum

### Company Exclusions
- Pure insurance tech (unless it has a meaningful wealth management product line)
- Pure banking/payments fintech with no wealth management focus
- Crypto-only companies (unless they have a wealth management platform)
